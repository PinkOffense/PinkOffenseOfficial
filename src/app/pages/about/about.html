Offensive security enthusiast focused on web application security, red teaming, and appsec.

I think like an attacker — mapping how systems break and turning findings into real improvements for defenders. My focus is understanding full attack paths, not isolated vulnerabilities. I work with Nmap, Burp Suite, Metasploit, Wireshark, and custom scripts to automate recon and exploitation. Most of my learning is hands-on: CTFs, labs, and platforms where I explore web apps, Active Directory environments, and chainable misconfigurations.

My background in software testing and quality assurance gave me a strong foundation in systematic thinking, edge-case analysis, and breaking things methodically. Now I apply that same mindset to security — finding the gaps others miss, understanding how defenses fail, and building reproducible attack chains. I'm currently pursuing CEH Practical and CompTIA CySA+ to formalize this transition.

This site documents that journey — write-ups, threat-modeling notes, tools I build, and study resources. Everything follows ethical hacking principles: legality, proper authorization, and the goal of strengthening systems. If you're into pentesting, red teaming, or appsec, you'll probably find something here that resonates.
